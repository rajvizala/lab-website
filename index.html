Welcome to TRAIL

The Trustworthy AI Lab (TRAIL) at the University at Buffalo (SUNY) is at the forefront of cutting-edge research in trustworthy AI systems, biometrics, and privacy-enhanced computing. Our mission is to develop robust, fair, and secure AI technologies that can be relied upon in critical applications, from healthcare diagnostics to biometric systems.

At TRAIL, we focus on addressing key challenges in AI trustworthiness, including privacy preservation, bias mitigation, and fairness in machine learning. Our interdisciplinary team combines expertise in computer science, data science, and ethics to create AI solutions that are not only technologically advanced but also socially responsible. Current projects include developing privacy-preserving algorithms for medical data analysis, including innovative work on sleep apnea diagnostics.

Our lab collaborates with industry partners and academic institutions worldwide, organizing workshops at prestigious conferences such as CVPR, ECCV, and ICCV. These events cover critical topics like bias in AI, privacy-enhanced computing, and advanced biometric technologies.

TRAIL is led by Dr. Nalini K. Ratha, an Empire Innovation Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering. Under his guidance, our team of researchers and students works on projects that push the boundaries of what's possible in trustworthy AI, privacy-preserving computing, and biometric systems.

Key Research Areas

  • Trustworthy AI systems
  • Fairness and bias mitigation in AI
  • Secure and robust biometric systems
  • Computer vision for societal impact
  • Ethical AI and responsible innovation

Current Projects

  • Creating explainable AI models for critical decision-making
  • Designing privacy-preserving biometric authentication methods
