Welcome to TRAIL

The Trustworthy AI Lab (TRAIL) is led by Dr. Nalini K. Ratha, a distinguished Research Staff Member at the IBM Thomas J. Watson Research Center in Yorktown Heights, New York. Dr. Ratha spearheads cutting-edge research in biometrics systems security, performance evaluation, and trustworthy AI systems.

With a career spanning decades, Dr. Ratha has made significant contributions to the field of biometrics and AI. His research initially focused on enhancing the security and performance of biometrics systems. In recent years, he has expanded his focus to building robust and trustworthy AI systems.

Dr. Ratha has been instrumental in organizing numerous workshops at prestigious conferences such as CVPR, ECCV, and ICCV, addressing critical topics like bias, blockchain, and disguised faces in the wild. During CVPR 2020, he co-organized a workshop on Trustworthy Vision Systems, further cementing his role as a thought leader in the field.

His academic contributions are equally impressive. Dr. Ratha has co-edited several influential books, including "Domain Adaptation for Visual Understanding" (Springer, 2020), "Advances in Biometrics: Sensors, Algorithms and Systems," and "Automatic Fingerprint Recognition Systems." He also co-authored the popular textbook "A Guide to Biometrics Selection and System Design."

Throughout his career, Dr. Ratha has been associated with leading biometrics conferences, serving in various capacities from general co-chair to program co-chair. His expertise has been recognized through his service on editorial boards of prestigious journals like IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence and IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man and Cybernetics.
